# TzDraft Content Strategy

**Date:** 2026-03-31  
**Audience:** Solo founder / small marketing team  
**Purpose:** Build a repeatable content system that grows awareness, drives play sessions, improves retention, and supports the low-cost promotion plan.

**Related document:** `docs/marketing/SOLO_LOW_COST_PROMOTION_PLAN.md` covers channel selection, posting rhythm, and low-cost distribution. This content strategy focuses on what to publish, why it matters, and how each content type supports product growth.

---

## 1. Strategy Goal

TzDraft should use content to do 4 jobs at the same time:

1. Bring new players in through search and social discovery
2. Turn visitors into active players quickly
3. Give existing players reasons to return every week
4. Make tournaments, AI challenges, and friend invites easier to promote

This means the content strategy is not just "post often."

It is:

**Teach the game, create challenges, celebrate players, and direct every piece of content to one clear action inside TzDraft.**

---

## 2. Core Positioning

Use one clear content position across all channels:

**TzDraft is the online home of Tanzania Drafti for learning, practice, competition, and community.**

Every content piece should reinforce at least one of these meanings:

- TzDraft protects and explains official Tanzania Drafti
- TzDraft helps players improve through AI and guides
- TzDraft gives real competition through online matches and tournaments
- TzDraft connects friends and communities through playable challenges

---

## 3. Content Objectives

### Primary Objectives

1. Increase weekly active players
2. Increase tournament registrations
3. Increase AI challenge participation
4. Increase friend-match invites
5. Grow search traffic to `/learn` and `/rules`

### Secondary Objectives

1. Build trust in TzDraft as a serious local platform
2. Improve social proof with player stories and match results
3. Create reusable assets that can be repurposed across Facebook, WhatsApp, YouTube Shorts, and onsite articles

---

## 4. Audience Segments

### Casual Players

- Want quick matches and easy friend invites
- Respond well to rivalry posts, challenge posts, and simple onboarding content

### Competitive Players

- Care about rankings, tournaments, and strong opponents
- Respond well to bracket updates, winner recaps, and leaderboard content

### Learners

- Need rules, tactics, examples, and AI practice
- Respond well to tutorials, explainers, and puzzle posts

### Organizers and Community Leaders

- Need reasons to bring groups onto the platform
- Respond well to campus challenge ideas, tournament kits, and community spotlights

---

## 5. Content Pillars

TzDraft should organize content around 5 pillars.

### Pillar 1: Rules and Education

Purpose:
Build search traffic and trust while helping beginners understand the game.

Best formats:

- Rules explainers
- Beginner guides
- Common mistake posts
- FAQ posts
- "What happens in this position?" clips

Main destinations:

- `/rules`
- `/learn`

Best CTA:

**Read the rule, then try it in a match**

### Pillar 2: Training and Improvement

Purpose:
Turn curiosity into active sessions through AI and puzzles.

Best formats:

- AI level challenges
- Position puzzles
- Tactic breakdowns
- "Beat this level" posts
- Improvement series for beginners

Main destinations:

- `/game/setup-ai`

Best CTA:

**Train now and beat the next level**

### Pillar 3: Competition and Events

Purpose:
Make tournaments the weekly heartbeat of the brand.

Best formats:

- Tournament announcements
- Countdown reminders
- Bracket updates
- Winner recaps
- Match highlight clips

Main destinations:

- `/community/tournament`
- `/game/setup-online`

Best CTA:

**Join this week's tournament**

### Pillar 4: Community and Social Proof

Purpose:
Show that real people already play, compete, and return.

Best formats:

- Player spotlights
- Leaderboard updates
- Rivalry posts
- User quotes
- Campus or community challenge posts

Main destinations:

- `/leaderboard`
- `/game/setup-friend`

Best CTA:

**Challenge a friend and prove it**

### Pillar 5: Product Onboarding

Purpose:
Help new users understand what they can do on TzDraft and where to start.

Best formats:

- "How to start" posts
- Mode comparison posts
- Short walkthrough clips
- "If you are new, start here" guides

Main destinations:

- `/play`
- `/game/setup-ai`
- `/game/setup-friend`

Best CTA:

**Start playing in under 1 minute**

## 7. Channel Strategy

### Onsite Content

Role:
Own search traffic and provide evergreen content that social posts can point to.

Priority pages:

1. `/rules`
2. `/learn`
3. tournament recap pages or summary posts

Content style:

- Clear headings
- Swahili-first where possible
- Real game examples
- Short paragraphs
- One clear CTA to play or join

### Facebook

Role:
Main community distribution channel for announcements, explainers, and tournament promotion.

Best content:

- Image posts
- short clips
- event announcements
- result recaps

### WhatsApp

Role:
Retention and conversion channel for active or warm players.

Best content:

- Registration reminders
- AI challenge links
- Direct match invites
- Short winner updates

### YouTube Shorts

Role:
Discovery channel for gameplay, puzzles, and quick rule education.

Best content:

- 15 to 45 second clips
- Fast puzzle reveals
- Match highlights
- Rule mistakes explained visually

## 8. Editorial Principles

Every TzDraft content piece should follow these rules:

1. Use one clear idea per post
2. Use one clear CTA per post
3. Show the game visually whenever possible
4. Prefer Swahili-first messaging for local reach, with English support when useful
5. Teach with real examples, not abstract theory
6. Link content to a product action, not just passive reading
7. Reuse tournament and gameplay moments as future content

Avoid:

- Generic gaming quotes
- Overly broad motivational posts
- Low-quality AI filler articles
- Posts with multiple competing CTAs
- Long captions with no playable next step

## 9. Content Formats To Prioritize

### Evergreen Formats

- Rules pages
- beginner guides

- strategy explainers
- FAQ content

These build long-term SEO value.

### Recurring Formats

- Weekly AI challenge
- weekly tournament announcement
- tournament countdown
- weekly winner recap

### Social Formats

- Puzzle image posts
- short match highlight clips
- tag-a-friend rivalry posts

These are cheap to produce and easy to repeat.

## 10. Repurposing System

One strong content idea should become multiple assets.

Example:

**Main topic:** "Common promotion mistake in Tanzania Drafti"

Turn it into:

1. One `/learn` article
2. One Facebook explainer post
3. One WhatsApp short reminder
4. One YouTube Short clip
5. One puzzle or challenge image
6. One CTA sending players to practice vs AI

This keeps content production realistic for a solo founder.

---

## 11. Weekly Content Engine

Use this lightweight weekly system:

### Monday: Teach

- Publish one rule, tactic, or beginner lesson
- Drive users to `/learn` or `/rules`

### Tuesday: Challenge

- Publish one AI challenge or puzzle
- Drive users to `/game/setup-ai`

### Wednesday: Invite

- Publish one friend-match or rivalry post
- Drive users to `/game/setup-friend`

### Thursday: Announce

- Publish weekly tournament post
- Drive users to `/community/tournament`

### Friday: Prove

- Publish a reminder with proof, bracket tease, or winner image
- Drive registrations

### Saturday: Capture

- Run the event and save screenshots, quotes, and clips

### Sunday: Recap

- Publish winner recap, key moment, or lesson
- Drive anticipation for next week

---

## 12. Recommended Content Mix

For every 10 pieces of content:

1. 3 pieces should educate
2. 2 pieces should challenge
3. 2 pieces should promote events
4. 2 pieces should show community proof
5. 1 piece should explain product onboarding

This balance prevents TzDraft from feeling like either:

- only a classroom
- only a tournament poster
- or only a product ad

## 17. Final Strategy Summary

TzDraft does not need a complicated content machine.

It needs a focused one.

The strongest strategy is:

1. Build authority with rules and learning content
2. Drive activity through AI challenges and friend invites
3. Make tournaments the weekly content centerpiece
4. Turn every event into proof, clips, and story-driven posts
5. Reuse each strong idea across multiple channels

If this is done consistently, content becomes more than marketing.

It becomes the system that connects discovery, play, competition, and retention.
