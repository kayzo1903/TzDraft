# TzDraft Solo Low-Cost Promotion Plan

**Date:** 2026-03-31  
**Audience:** Solo developer / founder  
**Goal:** Grow TzDraft with very low cost by using the product's strongest existing features instead of relying on paid ads.

---

## 1. What TzDraft Should Promote First

TzDraft already has a strong niche position:

- Tanzania Drafti online
- Play online against real people
- Play against AI
- Invite friends to private matches
- Join tournaments
- Learn through articles and rules pages

This means the cheapest promotion strategy is:

**Teach + challenge + community + invite**

Do **not** market the site as just "a game website."  
Market it as:

**The online home of Tanzania Drafti.**

---

## 2. Best Low-Cost Growth Channels

### Tier 1: Start Here

Use these first because they are cheap, simple, and realistic for one person:

1. **Facebook Page + Facebook Groups**
2. **WhatsApp group or broadcast list**
3. **YouTube Shorts**
4. **SEO through rules, guides, and tournament pages**

### Tier 2: Add Later

Only add these after you have weekly activity:

1. Instagram Reels
2. TikTok
3. Telegram or Discord community

### What to Avoid Early

- Paid ads
- Trying to grow on every platform at once
- Long branding projects before activity exists
- Publishing many weak AI-generated articles

---

## 3. Positioning Message

Use one clear message everywhere:

**TzDraft is the online home of Tanzania Drafti where players can practice with AI, challenge friends, and join real tournaments.**

Short variants:

- **Play Tanzania Drafti online for free**
- **Challenge AI, invite friends, join tournaments**
- **Weekly Tanzania Drafti tournaments online**
- **Learn, play, and compete in one place**

---

## 4. Audience Segments

Focus on these 4 groups:

1. **Casual players**
   Want simple, familiar play and friend invites.
2. **Competitive players**
   Care about tournaments, leaderboard, and strong opponents.
3. **Learners**
   Need AI, rules, and beginner content.
4. **Community organizers**
   Can bring groups of players through tournaments and invitations.

---

## 5. Solo Promotion Funnel

Use this simple funnel every week:

1. Publish one useful or exciting post
2. Send people to one clear action
3. Get them into a match or tournament
4. Ask them to invite one friend
5. Turn the result into next week's content

Example:

- Post a puzzle or challenge clip
- Link to `/play` or `/game/setup-ai`
- Ask users to beat an AI level or join Saturday tournament
- Post winner or leaderboard recap
- Ask every participant to bring one rival next week

---

## 6. Weekly Posting Calendar

This is the best low-cost calendar for a solo developer. Keep it light and repeatable.

### Weekly Structure

**Monday: Learn Post**

- Topic: rule, tactic, beginner tip, or common mistake
- Goal: attract search and social shares
- CTA: visit `/learn` or `/rules`

Example post:
> Do you know when promotion happens in Tanzania Drafti? Learn the official rule and practice it on TzDraft.

**Tuesday: AI Challenge Post**

- Topic: "Can you beat AI level X?"
- Goal: get quick gameplay sessions
- CTA: visit `/game/setup-ai`

Example post:
> Today's challenge: beat AI level 5 without losing a king. Try it and share your result.

**Wednesday: Friend Match Post**

- Topic: challenge a friend
- Goal: invite-based growth
- CTA: visit `/game/setup-friend`

Example post:
> Tag the friend who says they can beat you in Drafti. Send them a private match link and settle it tonight.

**Thursday: Tournament Announcement**

- Topic: weekly event
- Goal: registrations
- CTA: visit `/community/tournament`

Example post:
> Saturday 8 PM EAT: TzDraft Weekly Tournament. Registration is open now.

**Friday: Reminder + Proof Post**

- Topic: countdown, current players, bracket tease, past winner
- Goal: increase trust and urgency
- CTA: register now

Example post:
> Registration closes tomorrow. Last week's winner returns to defend the title. Can anyone stop them?

**Saturday: Live Activity Post**

- Topic: event day
- Goal: active participation
- CTA: join live or follow the bracket

Example post:
> Tournament starts tonight. Brackets, live updates, and results are on TzDraft.

**Sunday: Recap Post**

- Topic: winner, best moment, lesson from the week
- Goal: social proof and retention
- CTA: prepare for next week

Example post:
> This week's champion won 4 straight matches. Next tournament opens Monday.

## 8. Feature-by-Feature Promotion Plan

Tie each promotion to one feature and one audience.

### A. Online Play

**Route:** `/game/setup-online`

**Who it attracts:**

- Competitive players
- Returning players
- People who want fast action

**Best angles:**

- Ranked and casual matches
- Real opponents
- Quick matchmaking

**Best content types:**

- Match clips
- "Play now" challenge posts
- Rivalry posts
- Win streak screenshots

**Best CTA:**

**Find a real opponent now**

**Low-cost strategy:**

- Post short clips of real matches
- Share "online now" momentum during peak hours
- Encourage users to post their wins in WhatsApp or Facebook comments

---

### B. Play vs AI

**Route:** `/game/setup-ai`

**Who it attracts:**

- Beginners
- Solo players
- Players online at quiet times

**Best angles:**

- Practice anytime
- 19 levels
- Improve before tournaments

**Best content types:**

- Daily AI challenge
- "Can you beat level X?" posts
- Beginner improvement tips
- Puzzle-style positions

**Best CTA:**

**Train now and beat the next level**

**Low-cost strategy:**

- Run daily or twice-weekly AI challenges
- Use AI progression as your easiest retention loop
- Offer a simple badge system in posts like "Level 5 cleared"

## 15. Final Advice

If you are alone, your best advantage is not budget. It is focus.

So keep the strategy simple:

1. Run one weekly tournament
2. Post one useful thing every day
3. Push one main CTA at a time
4. Turn every player into an inviter
5. Reuse every event as content

If you do this consistently for 8 to 12 weeks, you can grow TzDraft without heavy ad spend.
