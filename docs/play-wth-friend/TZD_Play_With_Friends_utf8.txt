The Big Picture — Why This Feature Matters              Play with Friends is not a minor feature. It is the single highest-retention mechanic in any competitive game platform. Chess.com knows this — their &apos;Challenge a Friend&apos; flow is one of the top three entry points for new users. People do not discover draughts alone; they discover it because a friend sent them a link.                                                                  Why It Matters                                Chess.com Evidence                                TzDraft Opportunity                                     Viral acquisition                                Every game invite is a referral. Friend receives a link, creates account, plays. Zero ad spend.                                Each TZD game link sent by a Tanzanian player reaches someone new to the platform. First exposure to TZD rules is in the context of a real game with a friend.                                     Retention anchor                                Players who play with friends churn 3–5x less than solo players. The social bond becomes the reason to return.                                TZD community is tight-knit nationally. Friend games become a reason to check TzDraft daily.                                     Guest conversion                                Chess.com allows guests to accept challenges. Many guests later register. Friend games are the #1 guest-to-registered conversion path.                                Guests can play without registering. Show them the platform. Convert them after the game.                                     Cultural fit                                N/A                                In Tanzania, draughts (Bao/Draughts culture) is social and communal. Playing with a known friend matches how the game is actually played in real life.                                       ✦             Core design principle:          The invite link is the product. Everything else — settings, clocks, rematch — exists to make the game that starts from that link as good as possible. Never add friction between &apos;I want to play with my friend&apos; and &apos;we are playing.&apos;                             2. The Complete User Flow — Every Path                  3.1 Primary Flow: Registered User Invites a Friend              This is the main flow. Every other flow branches from this one.                                       STEP 1 — Entry Point            —  User clicks &apos;Play with Friend&apos; from navbar, dashboard, or post-game screen                  ↓                         STEP 2 — Game Setup Panel            —  Configure the game before sending the invite                  ↓                         STEP 3 — Link Generated            —  Unique invite URL created. Share options shown.                  ↓                         STEP 4 — Waiting Room (Host)            —  Host waits. Sees their own avatar + empty opponent slot. Countdown or &apos;Waiting...&apos;                  ↓                         STEP 5 — Friend Receives Link            —  Opens URL in any browser. Sees game invite preview.                  ↓                         STEP 6 — Friend Joins            —  Logged in: joins immediately. Guest: plays as guest. New user: quick register.                  ↓                         STEP 7 — Both in Waiting Room            —  Both players visible. Settings confirmed. 3-2-1 countdown begins.                  ↓                         STEP 8 — Game Starts            —  Board appears. Clock running. Full PvP game under TZD rules.                  ↓                         STEP 9 — Game Ends            —  Result screen. Rematch button. Share game button. Analysis link. Play again options.                                3.2 Guest Flow (Critical for Viral Growth)              A guest receives the link from a friend. They have never used TzDraft. The entire experience must be frictionless:                                 Guest opens invite link → sees a branded preview page with: game settings, host name/avatar, &apos;You&apos;ve been challenged to a game of Tanzania Draughts!&apos;                   Two buttons: &apos;Play as Guest&apos; (one click, enter name) and &apos;Create Account&apos; (30 seconds, then auto-joins game)                   Guest enters a display name. No email, no password, no verification. Instantly in the waiting room.                   Game plays normally. Guest has full board functionality, chat, draw/resign.                   After game ends — show conversion prompt: &apos;Save your game history and rating — create a free account.&apos; Pre-fill with the guest name they used.                   Guest session tied to a temporary token stored in localStorage. If they create an account within 24 hours, game history transfers.                                    ✦             Guest UX rule:          Zero forms before the game. The only thing a guest types before playing is their display name — one field, one button. Any more friction and they leave.                                3.3 Multi-Use Room Link (The WhatsApp Group Feature)              Unlike chess.com&apos;s single-use links, TzDraft supports persistent room links that can be used repeatedly — perfect for sharing in a WhatsApp group or family chat:                                   Room link:             tzdraft.tz/room/abc123          — anyone who opens it can join as the next available opponent                   Room host is always White by default (configurable). First person to click join becomes Black.                   If a game is already in progress when a third person opens the link, they automatically become a spectator.                   After game ends, the room resets. Next person to open the link starts a new challenge.                   Room links expire after 7 days of inactivity or can be manually cancelled by the host.                                    👥             Why this matters for Tanzania:          WhatsApp groups are the primary communication channel. A single persistent link shared once in a family or club group enables ongoing play without anyone needing to generate new links each time.                                3.4 Rematch Flow              The rematch experience must be instant and feel natural — like flipping the board and resetting the pieces in a physical game:                                 Game ends. Result shown with animation.                   Both players see: [Rematch] [New Game] [Analysis] [Share] buttons.                   If one player clicks Rematch, the other sees a &apos;Rematch offered&apos; notification with Accept/Decline.                   On Accept — colors swap automatically (whoever was White is now Black). Same time control. Game starts with a 3-second countdown.                   If the second player closed the tab — send a browser notification or in-app notification: &apos;[Friend] wants a rematch. Click to rejoin.&apos;                   Rematch link remains valid for 10 minutes. After that, the room expires.                                           4. The Waiting Room — A Feature Chess.com Skips              Chess.com takes you straight to a blank board with &apos;Waiting for opponent...&apos; text. This feels cold and uncertain. TzDraft can do much better — a proper waiting room that builds anticipation and keeps the host engaged while they wait.                                5.1 Host Waiting Room State                     Your avatar + username on the left.          Pulsing &apos;Waiting...&apos; placeholder on the right.                     Game settings summary visible:          10+5 Rapid · Casual · Spectators: On                     Share options still accessible:          &apos;Resend Link&apos; button, copy link, WhatsApp shortcut                     Cancel button:          Returns host to lobby and invalidates the link                     Live link view count:          &apos;Link opened 2 times&apos; — tells the host their friend is looking at it                                5.2 Friend&apos;s Invite Preview Page              Before the friend joins, they see a dedicated preview page — not just a blank board. This page is the first impression of TzDraft for new users:                                   Header:          &apos;[HostName] has challenged you to Tanzania Draughts!&apos;                     Host avatar and rating shown:          Makes it feel personal. &apos;Playing against: Amina Hassan · Rating: 1340&apos;                     Game settings:          &apos;10 minutes + 5 second increment · Casual game · Spectators allowed&apos;                     Two clear CTAs:          &apos;Accept Challenge&apos; (big green button) and &apos;Learn How to Play&apos; (secondary link)                     Background:          Animated TZD board with pieces — gives flavour while they decide                     If already logged in:          Name shown, Accept goes straight to waiting room                     If guest:          &apos;Enter your name to play as guest&apos; — single input field below the Accept button                                5.3 Both Players in Waiting Room — Game About to Start              The moment the second player joins, both players see the same screen:                                   Both avatars visible:          White piece icon under host, Black piece icon under joiner (or as configured)                     3-second animated countdown:          3... 2... 1... Go! — builds excitement, prevents instant-start surprise                     Game settings confirmed:          Both players see what they are about to play                     &apos;Change Settings&apos; — host only:          Host can modify within the 3-second window. Restarts countdown.                      ✦             Why the countdown matters:          Chess.com starts the game the moment both players are present — players are sometimes mid-scroll when the clock starts. A 3-second countdown is respectful and creates a ritual feel, like a referee starting a match.                             6. In-Game Features — What Friend Games Add                  6.1 Live Chat Panel              Chess.com has no in-game chat. This is a significant gap — friends playing online still want to trash-talk, react, and communicate. TzDraft has chat as a first-class feature:                                   Position:          Collapsible side panel next to the board. On mobile: bottom drawer.                     Message types:          Free text (max 200 chars) + Quick reactions (👏 😬 🤔 😂 🤝 🔥)                     Move reactions:          Click any past move to react to it specifically: &apos;!! brilliant&apos; or &apos;? mistake&apos;                     Moderation:          Automatic profanity filter. One-tap report. Mute button hides chat without closing it.                     Guest chat:          Guests can chat — name shown as &apos;[GuestName] (Guest)&apos;                     Chat persists:          Chat history saved with the game record. Viewable in post-game analysis.                                    👥             TzDraft differentiator:          Support Swahili and English in chat. Auto-detect language and show chat in the sender&apos;s language. If host wrote in Swahili and opponent replies in English, both see both. This makes the feature feel locally built, not imported.                                6.2 Spectator Mode              Anyone who opens a friend game link while a game is in progress automatically becomes a spectator:                                   Board view:          Read-only board. Moves shown in real-time with a 5-second delay (anti-cheating).                     Spectator chat:          Separate spectator chat channel. Players cannot see spectator messages during the game.                     Live viewer count:          Both players see &apos;X watching&apos; — adds social pressure and excitement.                     Spectator analysis bar:          Show a position evaluation bar to spectators only (never to players). Shows who is winning without telling the players.                     Post-game:          Spectators can chat with players once game ends. Spectator view of the game record.                     Max spectators:          100 per game (WebSocket room limit). Club tournaments can use this for live broadcasts.                                6.3 Take-Back (Optional, Friend Games Only)              Not available in ranked or casual matchmaking. Available in friend games if both players agree in setup:                                   Host enables take-backs in game setup (off by default).                              Requesting:          Button appears during your opponent&apos;s turn: &apos;Request take-back.&apos; Sends a prompt to opponent.                     Opponent accepts or declines:          If accepted, last move reverted. If declined, game continues.                     Limit:          3 take-backs per player per game. Prevents abuse.                     Clock:          Time used on the retracted move is restored minus 5 seconds (penalty).                                6.4 Abort Rule (Friend-Games-Only Relaxation)              In ranked matchmaking, aborting is strictly controlled. In friend games the rules are more relaxed because both players know each other:                   Either player can abort within the first 2 moves with no rating consequence.                   After move 3, abort counts as resignation for the aborting player.                   If internet drops, the disconnect grace period is extended to 3 minutes for friend games (vs 60 seconds for ranked).                             7. Club admin creates the club. Invites members via link or username.                   Club has its own chat channel — persistent, not tied to individual games.                   Club leaderboard shows member rankings within the club.                                9.2 Club Use Cases                     School draughts club:          Teacher creates a club, shares the join link with students. Play from different cities.                     TZD Federation chapter clubs:          Each regional chapter has a club. Club vs Club matches (Phase 3).                     Workplace:          Office draughts league. Lunchtime club. Persistent leaderboard on the company Slack.                                9.3 Club Tournaments (Phase 3)                   Club admin creates a round-robin or Swiss tournament within the club.                   Automatic pairings. Players get notifications for their next game.                   Live tournament standings page.                   Winner gets a club trophy badge visible on their profile.                             10.
